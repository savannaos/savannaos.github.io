STEM Resources

Resources for parents and teachers. Per educational technology research, I've found the following advice useful

  • Begin with action over language: don't formalize too early or you risk boring them
  • Avoid overly defining the steps to take. Allow students to scope the project idea and solution, contributing to have a sense of personal ownership. Start with an open ended problem
  • Allow just in time teaching. This on-demand approach is necessary since you cannot plan every part of the project. Allow students and yourself to learn tools and pass on knowledge continually.
  • Allow time for scientific inquiry instead of jumping into the "correct" answer
  • Don't worry if you don't have all the materials you would like. Engineers solve problems under contraints, budget, and materials. Contrainsts foster creativity.
